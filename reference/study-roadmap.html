Study Roadmap

Reference · 1 h/day baseline · multi-year arc

Thesis under test: relational deep learning and foundation relational models are undervalued. This roadmap builds the foundations to test that claim rigorously — not to skip straight to hype.

Credible proof requires knowing what you are beating. Most ML still assumes a single flat table per task. RDL asks: what if we learn directly from the database graph? Your daily hour alternates reading, lessons, and hands-on reproduction.

Full detail: see the Expert Curriculum — 6 years, 240 lectures, 60+ papers in chronological order, quarterly checkpoints, and a Year 6 novel-research phase.

Six phases (one year each)

PhaseYearFocusExit criterion
1 · Tabular foundations Year 1 sklearn pipelines, grouped CV, leakage, XGBoost/LightGBM baselines, Grinsztajn benchmark literacy Beat or match published tree baselines on a flat-table task with clean evaluation
2 · Advanced tabular Year 2 FT-Transformer, TabNet, embedding strategies; when neural tabular wins and loses Articulate the three inductive biases (smoothness, rotation, uninformative features)
3 · Graph ML Year 3 PyG, message passing, heterogeneous & temporal graphs, sampling at scale Train a GNN on a medium graph benchmark from scratch
4 · Relational DL Year 4 Fey et al. RDL, RelBench tasks, relational entity graphs, temporal splits Reproduce a RelBench baseline; compare to manual feature engineering
5 · Foundation models Year 5 LM+GNN pre-training, Zahradník vision, in-context relational learners, research gap mapping FM baseline reproduced; ranked research proposals written
6 · Novel research Year 6 Hypothesis · ablations · write-up · public release Artifact (paper, leaderboard, or product) that tests the thesis

Daily hour (Phase 1 template — 1 h baseline)

  1. 15 min — Retrieval: re-do yesterday’s quiz or explain one concept aloud
  2. 25 min — Current lesson or primary paper section (linked from lesson)
  3. 20 min — Hands-on: notebook cell, benchmark run, or pipeline tweak

Extra time? Extend the practice block (reproduction, hyperparameter tuning) or read the paper’s appendix — do not advance to the next year’s material until the current exit exam is passed.

Rules that protect the thesis

  • Never claim RDL wins until tree + manual FE baselines are fairly tuned
  • Temporal splits on relational data — no time-travel leakage
  • One phase exit criterion at a time; depth beats breadth
  • Cite primary sources in every result you share publicly
