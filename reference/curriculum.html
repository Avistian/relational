Expert Curriculum

Reference · 6 years · 1 h/day baseline · 240 units (concept + lab) · papers in chronological order

North star: become research-grade expert in relational deep learning, then produce novel work that tests whether foundation relational models are undervalued. Years 1–4 build irreplaceable foundations. Year 5 maps the frontier. Year 6 is your contribution.

Pace: plan for at least 1 hour/day. Extra time on strong days deepens labs and papers — it does not replace year exit exams or let you skip phases.

Why 240 units span 6 years: a numbered row is a unit (concept + its lab/reproduction + quiz), not a single day. Reading the lessons alone would take ~2 years at 2–3/week — but the calendar is set by doing: ~42% of your hours go to hands-on reproduction (training GNNs, RelBench runs) and ~19% to the Year 6 research project. Units are concept-dense early (Y1–Y2) and become multi-week research milestones late (Y5–Y6).
