# Pacing model: 240 units, not 240 days

The user noticed the apparent mismatch: 240 lessons ÷ 365 ≈ 0.66 years, so at one lesson/day the plan reads like ~8 months, not 6 years. The objection is correct about *lessons* and wrong about *mastery*.

**Resolution (chosen: blend of "lessons as real pace" + "labs count as units").**
- A numbered row is a **unit** = concept + its lab/reproduction + quiz, *not* one day.
- Unit sizes vary: concept lessons 1–3 sessions; reproduction/lab units 5–20 sessions; checkpoints 3–8; Year 6 research units 10–40+.
- Time budget: lessons themselves are only ~6% of the ~2,160 hours. ~42% is hands-on reproduction, ~19% is the Year 6 research project, the rest reading/checkpoints/review.
- Density curve is front-loaded: Y1–Y2 concept-dense (~2–3 units/week), Y3–Y4 reproduction-dominated (~1/week), Y5–Y6 research-dominated (sparse multi-week milestones).
- Implication: reading all 240 lessons would take ~2 years; the extra four years are where reproduction skill and the research contribution form.
