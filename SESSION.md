# How to run a session with your tutor

Copy the opener below into chat at the start of each study session. Fill the brackets.
The tutor reads `CURRICULUM.md` (what's next), `NOTES.md` (where you stopped), and
`GLOSSARY.md` (terms you've mastered) automatically — you only supply the bracketed bits.

---

## Daily opener (copy this)

```
Session. I'm on Lesson [00X]. Last time I covered [topic]. I have ~[60] min today.
Start with retrieval, then input, then a lab. Be strict — quiz me before teaching.
```

---

## The three blocks (what each turn looks like)

**1. Retrieval (15 min) — cold, before reopening notes**
- You say: `Quiz me on the last lesson.`
- Tutor: 3–5 recall questions. You answer from memory first.

**2. Input (25 min) — one concept + its failure mode**
- You say: `Teach Lesson 00X` or `Walk me through §[N] of [paper].`
- Interrupt freely: `slower` · `give an example` · `why does that matter?`

**3. Practice (20 min) — you produce, tutor reviews**
- You say: `Give me the lab.` then paste your attempt: `Here's my attempt — review it.`
- A unit is DONE only when its lab runs and its quiz/checkpoint passes.

---

## Useful commands

| When | Say this |
|------|----------|
| Forgot prior material | `Re-quiz me on everything since the last checkpoint.` |
| Hit a checkpoint (every ~10 units) | `Run the checkpoint — grade me, don't let me skip.` |
| Stuck on a lab | `I'm stuck on X. Hint only, not the answer.` |
| Want depth (extra time) | `Go deeper: show me the math / ablation / original code.` |
| Test true understanding | `Let me teach this back; tell me what I got wrong.` |
| End of session | `Log where I stopped and which papers I finished.` |

---

## Rules the tutor enforces

- Cold retrieval **before** any teaching.
- No advancing past a failed checkpoint.
- Labs are mandatory — no "done" on reading alone.
- Stay on the vetted paper list (`RESOURCES.md`); ignore arXiv noise unless it sets SOTA, exposes a failure mode, or is a baseline you'll be measured against.
- Honesty about confusion > performed fluency.
