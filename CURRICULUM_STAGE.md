# Curriculum staging — candidate papers

Candidate papers surfaced by the quarterly currency sweep, awaiting review before promotion into [CURRICULUM.md](./CURRICULUM.md). Newest at the bottom. Each entry must pass at least one add-only-if test (new SOTA · exposes a failure mode · baseline you'll be measured against) and not be application-only or an incremental variant. Dedupe by arXiv id and title before appending.

- [A Fair Benchmarking of Deep Relational Database Learning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03659) — Akhter, Ajendla & Samad, 2026. First systematic, protocol-consistent benchmark of deep RDB learners across five databases; finds the Relational Transformer strongest and shows deep RDB methods beat TabPFN-2.5 even on single-table tasks, with diminishing returns past hop=1–2. Directly extends the fair-comparison/honest-baseline thread (TabReD → TabArena → BeyondArena) and is a baseline suite the thesis will be measured against. (staged 2026-07-15)
- [Context-Constrained Transfer Learning for Tabular Foundation Models via Data Distillation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04809) — Lin & Li, 2026. TL-ANDI tackles two concrete TFM failure modes — strict context-size limits and negative transfer under source/target distribution shift — via optimal-transport anchor selection plus distilled labels and residual calibration. Sharpens the Y2 Q3 "where in-context learning breaks" story with a principled transfer-learning fix. (staged 2026-07-15)
- [TabPack: Efficient Hyperparameter Ensembles for Tabular Deep Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05380) — Gorishniy, Kotelnikov, Rubachev & Babenko, 2026. From the TabM/FT-Transformer group: a single-run MLP ensemble that samples heterogeneous hyperparameters and selects members on the fly, matching extensively tuned prior methods out of the box (competitive on a laptop). A strong-default trained-DL baseline in the RealMLP/TabM line that any honest comparison must include. (staged 2026-07-15)
- [Surprisingly Simple and Effective Multi-Domain Graph Foundation Model through Graph-to-Table Alignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11374) — Hu et al., 2026. GTAlign is a text-free graph foundation model that reuses tabular foundation models by learning a graph-to-table alignment (unified graph encoder + community-guided continual pre-training + in-context inference), beating SOTA on node and graph classification. A novel, direct bridge from TFMs to structural/graph learning — the exact tabular↔relational seam the curriculum's thesis lives on. (staged 2026-07-15)
